Research

Most of my current work uses longitudinal plant- and product-level data to examine the role of trade and trade policy in determining firm-level outcomes.

  1. Firms and Place-Based Policy: Evidence from Special Economic Zones in the Philippines. Working Paper.

    Using twenty years of Philippine manufacturing plant-level data, I provide the first causal firm-level evaluation of SEZ policy using quasi-experimental and endogenous switching methods. SEZs raise firm size and productivity, but effects are highly heterogeneous — firms less likely to select into SEZs also benefit less. Benefits do not spill over to non-registered neighboring firms, and publicly governed zones are 60% less productive than private ones.

  2. No Change, No Gain: Exporting, Diversification, and Firm Growth in the Philippines (with Jesus Felipe and Nanditha Mathew). Working Paper.

    We link Philippine manufacturing plant-level data to the universe of firm-product export transactions to estimate the effects of exporting and export diversification on firm outcomes, correcting for selection via switching regressions and exclusion restrictions. Exporting alone is not growth-enhancing. Diversification raises output and employment, but has no impact on productivity.

  3. A Profile of the Largest 1,000 Firms in the Philippines and the Manufacturing Sector (with Jesus Felipe and Nanditha Mathew). AKI Working Paper Series, 2025.
  4. The IMF-World Bank Meetings and their Recommendations for Asia: an Assessment (with Jesus Felipe). AKI Policy Briefs, 2024.
